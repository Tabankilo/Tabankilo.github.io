A NEGATIVE result indicates lack of detectable virus and not necessarily absence of infection. A negative result in an infected individual could occur in the following situations:

a) Sample collection too early in the disease especially before the onset of symptoms
b) Sample collection too late in the disease
c) Inadequate sample collected
d) Poor sample handling and transportation
e) Mutated virus that cannot be detected by PCR
